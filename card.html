White Tiger

The white tiger is a pigmentation variant of the Bengal tiger.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera Tigris
  • Average Length: 9 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 12 years
  • Habitat: Grasslands and forest

The white tigers are distinctive due to the color of their fur. The white fur caused by a lack of the pigment pheomelanin, which is found in Bengal tigers with orange color fur. White male tigers reach weights of 200 to 230 kilograms and can grow up to 3 meters in length
